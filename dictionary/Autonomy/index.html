Autonomy

Definition

Autonomy (noun)

The right or condition of self-government, especially in a particular sphere or context.

Primary Meanings

  • 1. Freedom from external control or influence; independence.
    • Example: "The country's struggle for autonomy from colonial rule lasted decades."
    • Example: "The school was granted autonomy over its curriculum."
  • 2. The capacity of an individual to make an informed, uncoerced decision; self-determination.
    • Example: "Personal autonomy is crucial for individual freedom."
    • Example: "In medicine, patient autonomy is a foundational principle."
  • 3. A self-governing country or region.
    • Example: "Greenland has significant autonomy from Denmark."
    • Example: "The Basque region has fought for increased autonomy within Spain."

Synonyms

  • Independence
  • Self-rule
  • Sovereignty
  • Self-governance
  • Self-determination
  • Freedom

Antonyms

  • Dependency
  • Subjugation
  • Control
  • Oppression

Example Sentences

  • The company values employee autonomy and encourages decision-making at all levels.
  • His desire for autonomy led him to start his own business.
  • The university was granted autonomy to establish its own policies.
  • In psychology, autonomy is considered a fundamental need for personal growth.
  • Many regions in the world seek greater autonomy to control their resources.

Origin

The word "autonomy" originates from the Greek word autonomia, where auto means "self" and nomos means "law." It first appeared in English in the early 17th century, used to denote self-governance and later expanded to signify personal freedom and independence.

Usage Notes

"Autonomy" is often used in political, social, and psychological contexts. Politically, it refers to self-governance of a region or state. In social contexts, it emphasizes the freedom of individuals or groups from external control. In psychology, it reflects the capacity for self-direction and independent decision-making. "Autonomy" is commonly associated with rights and ethics, especially in discussions of personal freedom, medical ethics, and self-empowerment.
