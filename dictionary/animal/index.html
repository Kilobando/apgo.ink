Animal

Definition

Animal (noun)

A living organism that feeds on organic matter, typically having specialized sense organs and nervous system, and able to respond rapidly to stimuli.

Primary Noun Meanings

  • 1. Any member of the kingdom Animalia, encompassing a wide range of living organisms from mammals to insects, typically characterized by voluntary movement and specialized sensory organs.
    • Example: "Humans are classified as animals under the kingdom Animalia."
    • Example: "The zoo is home to over 100 species of animals from around the world."
  • 2. A brute, often used colloquially to describe someone lacking civility or reason.
    • Example: "He behaved like an animal when he got angry."
    • Example: "She thought of him as nothing more than an animal after witnessing his cruelty."

Alternative Forms

Animal (adjective)

  • Relating to, characteristic of, or derived from animals.
    • Example: "She studies animal behavior for her research."
    • Example: "The organization focuses on animal rights and welfare."

Synonyms

  • Creature
  • Organism
  • Beast
  • Living Being

Antonyms

  • Plant
  • Object
  • Mineral

Example Sentences

  • Humans and other mammals are considered animals.
  • The children were excited to see the animals at the zoo.
  • Some people treat others like animals without empathy or respect.
  • Environmental groups are concerned about animal habitats being destroyed.
  • She believes in protecting animal rights and ensuring they are treated humanely.

Origin

The word "animal" originated from the Latin word animalis, meaning "having breath" or "living." The term entered Old French as animal and was adopted into Middle English. Over time, it came to signify a living organism that feeds on organic matter and is capable of voluntary movement.

Usage Notes

"Animal" is primarily used to refer to members of the kingdom Animalia in scientific and general contexts. In casual language, it may be used figuratively to describe someone acting in a wild or uncivilized manner. As an adjective, "animal" can relate to characteristics, behaviors, or products derived from animals, such as "animal rights" or "animal instincts."
