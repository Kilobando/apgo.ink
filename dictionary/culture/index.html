Culture

Definition

Culture (noun)

The shared values, beliefs, practices, customs, and behaviors of a particular group, society, or community.

Primary Noun Meanings

  • 1. The collective customs, arts, social institutions, and achievements of a particular nation, people, or other social group.
    • Example: "Italian culture is known for its contributions to art, architecture, and cuisine."
    • Example: "The culture of the region includes unique musical traditions."
  • 2. The attitudes, values, and practices that characterize an organization or workplace.
    • Example: "The company's culture encourages innovation and collaboration."
    • Example: "A positive workplace culture can increase productivity."
  • 3. A particular type of intellectual or artistic activity within society.
    • Example: "He has a deep appreciation for literary culture."
    • Example: "The museum celebrates the city’s art and culture."
  • 4. The cultivation of bacteria, tissue cells, etc., in an artificial medium for scientific research.
    • Example: "A culture of bacteria was grown in the lab for the study."
    • Example: "The scientist carefully monitored the culture to observe growth."

Synonyms

Antonyms

Example Sentences

  • "The diversity of global cultures makes travel an enriching experience."
  • "Corporate culture plays a significant role in employee satisfaction."
  • "He is a product of his community's culture and values."
  • "In biology, they study the growth of cell cultures in petri dishes."
  • "Pop culture influences youth across many countries."

Origin

The term "culture" originates from the Latin word cultura, meaning "cultivation" or "tending." Originally, it referred to the cultivation of land or crops. Over time, it evolved to mean the cultivation of knowledge, ideas, or values within a society, particularly in the context of social and intellectual refinement.

Usage Notes

The term "culture" has broad applications. In anthropology and sociology, it refers to the shared beliefs and behaviors within a group. In popular discourse, "culture" might refer to customs, values, or practices unique to a specific group or place (e.g., "youth culture"). In biology, "culture" describes the growth of microorganisms or cells in a controlled environment. Additionally, "culture" as a term is increasingly used in corporate contexts to define the environment and values that shape an organization.

It is one of the most flexible words in English, adaptable across disciplines yet complex in its exact meaning, often requiring context to understand its full implication.
