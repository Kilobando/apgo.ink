Example

Definition

Example (noun)

A thing characteristic of its kind or illustrating a general rule.

Primary Noun Meanings

  • 1. A representative form or pattern, often used to illustrate a point or clarify a rule.
    • Example: "The teacher used an example to explain the concept of gravity."
    • Example: "The museum displayed a perfect example of ancient pottery."
  • 2. A person or thing that serves as a model to be imitated.
    • Example: "He set a good example for the other students."
    • Example: "Her courage was an example to everyone in the community."
  • 3. A punishment intended to discourage others from similar behavior (archaic).
    • Example: "The criminal was made an example of to warn others."

Alternative Forms

Example (verb - to example, examples, exampled)

  • To serve as an example or to illustrate by giving an example.
    • Example: "He exampled the correct method by showing his own work."
    • Example: "The teacher exampled how to solve the equation step by step."

Example Sentences

  • The teacher provided an example to clarify the concept.
  • This painting is an example of modern art.
  • She set a good example for the younger students.
  • The architect showed an example of the new design to the clients.
  • For instance, this sentence is an example of how to use the word "example."
  • The poor man's fate was an example to others not to defy the king (archaic use).

Origin

The word "example" originated from the Latin word exemplum, meaning "sample" or "specimen." It was later adopted into Old French as essemple and entered Middle English as example. The meaning evolved to represent something that serves as a pattern or model to be imitated.

Usage Notes

"Example" is versatile in its application. It is often used in academic and professional writing to clarify points or illustrate concepts. Phrases such as "for example" or "as an example" commonly introduce examples to support an argument. Additionally, "example" has an archaic meaning as a warning or punitive model, as in "he was made an example of." In some contexts, "example" can even function as a verb, meaning to illustrate through example.
