God

Definition

God (noun)

Primary Noun Meanings

  • 1. In monotheistic religions, the supreme being, creator, and ruler of the universe; often referred to with reverence and seen as omnipotent, omniscient, and omnipresent.
    • Example: "Many people believe that God watches over them and guides their lives."
  • 2. In polytheistic religions, one of several deities worshiped, each often associated with specific aspects of life, nature, or human activity.
    • Example: "Ancient Greeks worshiped many gods, including Zeus, the king of the gods."
  • 3. An image, idol, or representation of a deity worshiped as a god.
    • Example: "They placed offerings in front of the god to show respect and seek blessings."
  • 4. A person or thing accorded supreme importance or revered as a god.
    • Example: "For some fans, their favorite musician is like a god to them."
    • Example: "Money has become a god for many in today's society."

Adjective Form

Godlike (adjective) – having qualities that resemble those attributed to God, such as power, beauty, or wisdom.

  • Example: "The athlete's strength was almost godlike in its power and precision."
  • Example: "She possessed a godlike calm and wisdom that inspired everyone around her."

Synonyms

  • Deity
  • Divinity
  • Creator
  • Supreme Being
  • Almighty

Antonyms

  • Mortal
  • Human
  • Creature

Example Sentences

  • "Christians believe in one God who is all-powerful and all-knowing."
  • "In Hinduism, different gods represent various aspects of life, like Vishnu, the preserver, and Shiva, the destroyer."
  • "To them, their celebrity idol is practically a god."
  • "The temple held a magnificent statue of their god, surrounded by offerings."
  • "Her presence exuded a godlike calm that calmed everyone around her."

Origin

The word "God" has roots in the Old English word god, derived from the Proto-Germanic gudan. It is related to words in other Germanic languages, such as Dutch god and German Gott. In many cultures and religions, "God" is associated with a supreme deity, representing the source of all creation and power.

Usage Notes

In religious contexts, "God" is typically capitalized when referring to the monotheistic deity of Abrahamic religions (e.g., Judaism, Christianity, and Islam). However, in polytheistic contexts, it may be lowercase (e.g., "the Greek gods"). In a broader or metaphorical sense, "god" can refer to anything that is held in the highest esteem, such as fame, wealth, or success.
