Human

Definition

Human (noun)

A member of the species Homo sapiens, distinguished from other animals by higher mental development, articulate speech, and an upright stance.

Primary Noun Meanings

  • 1. An individual belonging to the species Homo sapiens.
    • Example: "Every human has the right to freedom and dignity."
    • Example: "Researchers are studying the effects of space travel on the human body."
  • 2. A person, especially in contrast with a machine, animal, or divine being.
    • Example: "Despite technological advances, humans still crave social interaction."
    • Example: "The robot’s goal is to assist, not replace, humans."

Alternative Forms

Human (adjective)

  • Relating to or characteristic of people as opposed to machines or animals.
    • Example: "Empathy is a fundamental human trait."
    • Example: "There are limits to human endurance."
  • Having human weaknesses and imperfections.
    • Example: "To err is human."
    • Example: "His reaction was very human under the circumstances."

Synonyms

  • Person
  • Individual
  • Mortal
  • Homo sapiens
  • Soul

Antonyms

  • Animal
  • Machine
  • God
  • Robot

Example Sentences

  • The preservation of nature is crucial for human survival.
  • She showed a human level of empathy for the struggling students.
  • Despite its AI, the robot lacks true human understanding.
  • Scientists have long studied human anatomy to better understand the body.
  • To forgive is human, to forget is divine.

Origin

The word "human" originates from the Latin word humanus, meaning "of or belonging to man." This Latin root is derived from homo, meaning "man" or "person." Over time, it came to represent characteristics, qualities, and behaviors unique to human beings.

Usage Notes

The word "human" is versatile in its applications. As a noun, it specifically refers to a member of the species Homo sapiens. As an adjective, it describes traits, qualities, and behaviors that are characteristic of humans, often used to distinguish from animals, machines, or divine beings. Phrases like "to err is human" or "human error" emphasize human limitations and imperfections.
