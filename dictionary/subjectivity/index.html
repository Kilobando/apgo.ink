Subjectivity

Definition

Subjectivity (noun)

The quality of being based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions, rather than external facts or evidence.

Primary Noun Meanings

  • 1. The state or quality of being subjective; reliance on personal interpretation rather than objective evidence.
    • Example: "The review was marked by a high level of subjectivity, reflecting the critic's personal preferences."
    • Example: "Art often involves subjectivity, as interpretations can vary widely among viewers."
  • 2. A personal viewpoint or interpretation that is shaped by individual feelings and biases.
    • Example: "Her subjectivity influenced her judgment, making her unable to evaluate the issue objectively."
    • Example: "In philosophy, subjectivity is often explored to understand personal perspectives and self-awareness."

Synonyms

  • Bias
  • Personal view
  • Individuality
  • Perspective
  • Impression

Antonyms

  • Objectivity
  • Impartiality
  • Fact
  • Neutrality

Example Sentences

  • The judges were criticized for allowing subjectivity to affect their decisions.
  • His subjectivity made it difficult for him to assess the situation fairly.
  • Many aspects of literature analysis rely on subjectivity because interpretations can vary.
  • In science, subjectivity is generally minimized in favor of objective data.

Origin

The word "subjectivity" is derived from the Latin subjectivus, meaning "of the subject." The term evolved through medieval Latin and entered English in the 17th century, with a primary focus on personal perception and individual viewpoint. Its modern usage often contrasts with "objectivity."

Usage Notes

"Subjectivity" is frequently used in contexts where personal feelings or individual perspective are involved, especially in fields like psychology, philosophy, and the arts. It contrasts with "objectivity," which refers to impartiality and factual observation. In discussions on ethics, journalism, or science, "subjectivity" often indicates bias or the influence of personal preference.
