In development · Football

A ranking that shows its work.

The APGO Football Index will be an independent, position-aware ranking of elite players. No mystery panel. No reputation bonus. Every score will ship with its method, data window and uncertainty.

The problem

Player lists exist. A common standard does not.

Awards mix team success, memory, visibility and taste. Data models often hide their weights. The APGO Index is designed to make judgment inspectable.

01

Position aware

Goalkeepers, centre-backs, creators and finishers are judged against the responsibilities of their role before being compared across roles.

02

Context adjusted

Opponent quality, competition level, possession environment and match state change what a raw action means.

03

Auditable

Each release will publish definitions, weights, minimum minutes, data coverage, revisions and known blind spots.

Proposed methodology · v0.1

Five layers, one visible score.

The first model will use a rolling 365-day window and position-specific baselines. The published ranking should not begin until data coverage, corrections and reproducibility are strong enough to defend.

Important: these weights are a public proposal, not a live ranking. APGO will test them against historical seasons and publish changes before release.

Publication rules

What makes the index credible

The method is not finished when the formula runs. Governance, corrections and clear limits are part of the product.

Rule 01

No unexplained movement

Every release will explain major rises and falls, including changes caused by model revisions rather than new performance.

Rule 02

Separate model from editorial list

A data score and an editor’s judgment are different products. APGO will label each instead of blending them invisibly.

Rule 03

Publish uncertainty

Low minutes, weak league coverage and role ambiguity reduce confidence. The interface will show that instead of pretending every decimal is equal.

Rule 04

Archive every edition

Previous rankings, weights and corrections will remain available so readers can see how the system and its conclusions changed.

Build sequence

From idea to public reference

Calling a ranking “official” would require authority from a governing body. APGO’s achievable goal is different: become the most transparent independent reference.

Phase 1

Data and backtesting

Choose licensed or genuinely open data, normalize competitions, define roles and test several past seasons for obvious bias.

Phase 2

Pilot publication

Release a limited top 25 with confidence bands, a complete methods paper and a public correction channel.

Phase 3

Quarterly index

Expand coverage only after reliability is proven. Add player pages, trend histories and model-change notes.

Build with us

Fund an open football standard.

Support will go toward data access, research, engineering and independent editorial review—not toward buying a preferred result.
